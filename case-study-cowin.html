Designing for a Nation in Crisis: The Co-WIN Booking Platform

Leading the transformation of API chaos and public panic into a clear, trustworthy experience that facilitated over 2 crore vaccine certificate downloads and drove 7-8k daily app installs.

Role: Lead Product Designer Company: Eka.Care Outcome: 2 Crore+ Downloads, #1 on Google Play Store Skills: Crisis-Driven UX, Rapid Prototyping, Cross-Functional Leadership

The Challenge: Imposing Clarity on Chaos

The Business Problem: In 2021, at the peak of India's COVID-19 crisis, Eka.Care made a strategic decision to integrate the government's Co-WIN APIs. The executive team's mandate was extreme: deliver a public-facing solution in a matter of days to capture a massive, anxious user base. There was zero room for error or iterative cycles.

The User Problem: The user's reality was one of extreme panic. The official government portal was overwhelmed, reliable information was scarce, and public trust was at an absolute low. They didn't need a clever UI; they needed an unambiguous, reliable lifeline to a vaccine.

Deconstructing the Chaos

My first responsibility as the lead designer was to bridge the high-level business goal with the on-the-ground technical reality. I took ownership of deconstructing the Co-WIN API's "chaos" into specific design challenges the team could tackle head-on:

  • Inconsistent Data: The API would return a hospital's name but not its address, requiring components that could gracefully handle missing information without appearing broken.
  • Vague Error States: A booking failure could mean "slot taken" or "API server down." We had to design a single, reassuring message for all backend failures that wouldn't cause more panic.
  • Undocumented Dependencies: The Dose 2 booking flow had hidden dependencies on Dose 1 data, which required my close collaboration with engineering to map and solve for in the UI.

I translated these technical realities into a clear set of user flows and tangible design artifacts. This served as our single source of truth, aligning the entire team and de-risking the project to meet our aggressive timeline.

The Core Insight: In a Crisis, Clarity is the Ultimate Feature

My analysis led to a critical hypothesis: standard design wisdom of "less is more" was dangerously wrong for this context. For a user in a state of panic, ambiguity is the enemy. Every unanswered question increases anxiety and erodes trust.

From Insight to Action: Design Pillars of Trust

1. A Contrarian Bet: Why Showing 'Nothing' Built Trust

One of my most crucial—and initially debated—decisions was to display nearby hospitals even when they had zero available slots. I successfully argued that in a low-trust environment, proving our system was comprehensive was more important than adhering to conventional 'clean' UX. Seeing a familiar but unavailable hospital reassured users our app was working correctly, not broken. This decision was a clear demonstration of prioritizing user psychological safety over UI tidiness and was pivotal in building long-term trust.

2. Pragmatism Under Pressure: A Key Trade-Off

To deliver at speed, we made a crucial trade-off in close collaboration with product and engineering. Instead of building a complex, resource-intensive real-time slot counter, we opted for a simpler "Last updated X minutes ago" timestamp. This was the most pragmatic way to provide the necessary level of trust to the user without jeopardizing our aggressive launch timeline.

3. The Final Handshake: Designing the Confirmation as a Contract

Finally, the confirmation screen was designed to be the ultimate source of truth. It contained the hospital's full address, a list of required documents, and pre-arrival instructions. We treated this screen as a contract with the user, designed to eliminate any day-of-appointment anxiety and ensure a successful vaccination.

The Impact: Market Dominance and a Foundation of Trust

Quantifiable Impact:

  • Achieved #1 ranking in the Health & Fitness category on the Google Play Store.
  • Facilitated over 2 Crore (20 million) vaccine certificate downloads.
  • Drove a sustained 7,000-8,000 new app installations daily at its peak.
  • Became the top-ranking non-government entity for the "Co-WIN" search term on Google.

Leadership Reflection:

  1. On Leading Through Influence: This project solidified my ability to drive strategy and align cross-functional teams using design artifacts as the central point of truth. My role evolved from executing a brief to proactively shaping the plan itself.
  2. On Designing for Risk: I learned to design for mission-critical systems where the cost of failure is immense. We treated every design choice—from wording to error messages—as a risk-mitigation strategy to protect both the user and the company's credibility.

Legacy & Future Vision:

The success of this feature became the bedrock of Eka.Care's credibility. To capitalize on this momentum, I developed and presented a V2 product roadmap that outlined how we could evolve this crisis-response feature into a comprehensive family health management hub. This strategic vision turned a temporary solution into a long-term, foundational asset for the company.
