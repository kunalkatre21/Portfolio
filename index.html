Guiding Principles

Clarity is the Challenge

For me, a "design challenge" isn't a difficult UI. It's ambiguity. My core skill is turning vague directives and chaotic environments into clear, actionable, and well-framed problems.

Systems over Symptoms

I solve problems at the highest possible level of abstraction. I'd rather build a robust theming "contract" that prevents future issues than fix a single component's color.

Native Components First

After seeing custom components fail, my philosophy is firm: leverage high-quality native libraries first. Only build custom when there's no alternative. This raises quality and developer velocity.

Pragmatism over Ego

The product's success is more important than my ideas being "right." I believe in shipping, measuring, and adapting. I'm especially resilient in environments where things are constantly rebuilt or ignored.
